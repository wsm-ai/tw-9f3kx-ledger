# The Weight — Physical Build Checklist

Everything needed to build the physical side of the promise ledger: four jars,
three metals, the stamping tools, and how it all maps to the app. Buy the cheap
starter set now; upgrade later if the project sticks.

> Prices are rough and change constantly — confirm each listing before buying.
> Nothing here is endorsed; verify gauge, size, pack count, and metal-hardness
> ratings on the actual product page.

---

## 1. The four jars

The system has four jars — **Open · Standing · Kept · Broken**. The goal is
vessels that feel intentional/ceremonial without spending hundreds. Plan:
**three matched clear cork-stopper jars** for Open/Standing/Kept, and **one
obscured (amber or smoked) jar** for Broken so the "let go" pile is present but
not on display.

### The three clear jars (Open · Standing · Kept)
- **Buy:** four — er, *three* — matching **clear glass jars with cork stoppers**,
  one size, ~25–34 oz, **wide-mouth** (you need to drop discs in and pull them
  out, so avoid narrow bottle necks).
- **Why cork:** reads as apothecary/potion, not kitchen storage; cheap; and cork
  survives travel better than a glass stopper (won't shatter if dropped).
- **Where:** eBay / Walmart / Target "glass jars with cork lids" — sold as 4-packs
  or as singles you can match. ~$15–25 for the set.
- **Tip:** buy the *same* size for all three so fill levels compare honestly.
  (You also get exact counts from the app, but matched jars make the shelf read
  true at a glance.)

### The obscured jar (Broken)
- **Buy:** one **amber** (or smoked/frosted) glass jar with a cork stopper,
  same approximate size as the other three.
- **Why:** amber reads as "archived / medicinal," softening the broken jar rather
  than shaming it. You'll see the fill level but not read individual discs through
  it — that's the intended "set down, not on display" effect.
- **Where:** amber cork-top bottles/jars are cheap and everywhere (eBay, craft and
  bottle suppliers). ~$5–10 for one.

**Starter jars total: ~$20–35 for all four.**

### Later upgrade (only if the project lasts)
- **Ground-glass-stopper apothecary jars** (clear / violet / amber) — heavier,
  more reliquary-like, sourced new (lab-supply) for matched sizes or antique
  (Etsy/eBay) for patina with size variance.
- **Infinity Jars apothecary set** — all-glass, thick, near-black UV-violet,
  travels well. Beautiful but **expensive (hundreds for four)** — a "someday"
  purchase, not a starter.

## 2. The discs — three metals, one per weight tier

Round disc blanks, **¾"–1"**, around **14 gauge** (heavy enough to feel
substantial and hold a deep stamp). One metal per tier:

| Tier | Metal | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Ordinary | **Aluminum** | Cheapest; also your practice metal — buy the biggest pack. |
| Serious | **Brass** | ~1"/25mm round blanks, no hole, packs of ~20–30. |
| Standing | **Copper** | Heaviest; thick deburred 1" blanks. Patinas over time — fitting for "forever." |

- Buy **three separate round-disc packs** (mixed-metal packs are mostly bracelet
  strips, not coins).
- Get **extra aluminum** to practice on before stamping real brass/copper.
- **~$30 combined** for the three packs.

---

## 3. Hammer + block

The instrument you touch every time you bind a promise.

- **Brass stamping hammer, ~1 lb** — brass disperses the strike evenly, prevents
  double impressions, and protects your stamps. Better than a steel claw hammer.
- **Recommended starter:** a **brass-hammer + 4"×4"×½" hardened steel bench block
  set** (e.g. HimaPro) — durable two-piece base in one purchase (~$25–35).
- **Refined upgrade:** **ImpressArt Ergo Angle** brass hammer (1 lb, replaceable
  face) for cleaner, squarer strikes.
- The thick steel block is heavy, stable, and effectively indestructible — good
  for both stamping quality and travel.

---

## 4. The imprints (kept plain)

Let the jars and metals carry the symbolism; keep the lettering clean.

- **Buy:** a plain steel **number + letter punch set** rated for **brass and
  copper** (not just aluminum), 3mm/⅛" or ¼". E.g. OWDEN 36-pc (HRC 58–62) or
  NEIKO 02624.
- **You need:** letters **M** and **O**, digits **0–9**, and a **hyphen** if the
  set has one (otherwise leave a small gap). ~$15–20.
- **Confirm the hardness rating** covers copper/brass — the harder of your metals.

## 4b. Favor tokens (optional — bearer IOUs)

If you use favor tokens (discs you hand out as "I owe you one favor"), they need
two extra physical things beyond a normal disc:

- **A custom maker's-mark stamp.** A single steel punch cut with your own unique
  glyph/monogram — order one custom (jewelry-supply and stamp-makers do these).
  Because it can't be bought off a shelf, it's the hardest layer for someone to
  forge. Stamp it on every favor disc.
- **An engraving pen** (~$10–20, e.g. a diamond-tip or rotary engraver). You
  **hand-engrave** the app-generated **10-digit verification number** on the
  **back** of each favor disc. Hand engraving (vs. stamping) is hard to reproduce,
  and the number only matters if it matches your ledger — so a forged disc fails
  verification.

Favor disc layout:
- **Front:** `F-047` (favor prefix + number) + your custom maker's mark.
- **Back:** the 10-digit verification number, hand-engraved.

The app tells you the number to engrave (on the entry and the stamping worklist),
checks it when you redeem, and blocks double-redemption. See the favor section in
README.md for the full anti-forgery rationale and its honest limits.

## 5. Making the stamp readable

- **Metal-stamp enamel marker** (ImpressArt / OWDEN). Rub into the stamped grooves,
  wipe the face — the label stays dark and legible. Black or white for contrast on
  brass/copper. ~$8. (Some kits include these — check before buying separately.)

---

## 6. Carrying it (bring-anywhere kit)

- **Canvas or leather tool roll** for the punches — protects the stamp faces and
  suits the ritual feel.
- **Small padded / foam hard case** to hold the steel block, brass hammer, disc
  packs, and (wrapped) any jar you travel with.
- **Leather strike pad** — quiets the strike and protects surfaces away from home.
- **Reality check:** block + brass hammer weigh a few pounds. This is a "bring it
  deliberately" kit, not pocketable. For true portability, drop to a 2–3" block
  and a lighter hammer (at some cost to stamping ease). ~$20–30 for roll + case.

---

## 7. Optional — extra weight

Your metals already make the gradient (copper heaviest). If you want more heft in
any jar, drop in a few **steel washers** from a hardware store (~$3–5).

## How the disc is stamped (the conventions)

Every promise in the app gets a disc label + a metal, shown on its entry and in
the **Export discs for engraving (.csv)** worklist.

- **Label:** `M-047` (to **m**yself) or `O-047` (to **o**ther) + the disc number.
- **Metal:** Aluminum / Brass / Copper, by the weight tier you chose.
- **Color/recipient** is already in the label (M vs O), so no paint dot needed.

### Workflow
1. Make the promise in the app → it assigns the label and tells you the metal.
2. Grab a blank in that metal; stamp the label (`M-047`); ink the grooves; wipe.
3. Hit **Mark stamped** in the app.
4. Drop the disc in the jar matching its status (Open / Standing / Kept / Broken).
5. When a promise changes (kept / broken / promoted), move the disc to the new jar.
   - **Promotion note:** if an Open promise becomes Standing, its disc may be in a
     lighter metal than copper. Either re-stamp a copper disc and retire the old
     one, or keep the original as a record of where the promise began.
6. The app's **unstamped worklist** banner + evening reminder tells you which
   discs still need making.

## Buy-before-you-stamp reminders
- Confirm the **punch set is rated for brass and copper**, not just aluminum.
- Match the **three clear jars by size**; make the **fourth amber** for Broken.
- Favor **wide-mouth** jars — you'll be adding/removing discs constantly.
- **Practice on aluminum** before real brass/copper — first strikes are uneven.
- Glass is glass: wrap jars when traveling; cork helps but isn't drop-proof.
