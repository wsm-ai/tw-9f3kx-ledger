# The Weight — Physical Build & Inventory

The physical side of the promise ledger: the jars, the metals, the tools, how a
disc is made, and what to upgrade as the practice deepens. This reflects the
**current** system (front/back layout, the dead-vs-memorial distinction, the
hand-engraved keyword and fate-glyph).

## 1. The current disc conventions

How a disc is laid out and made, matching the app.

### The two faces
- **Front — the fate.** The identity label (`M-###` to self, `O-###` to other,
  `F-###` favor) stamped in the center, with the **fate-glyph engraved around /
  enclosing it**, the **keyword hand-engraved above** the number, and — on favors —
  the **10-digit verification number** hand-engraved on the front.
- **Back — the maker's mark**, enlarged into a sigil that seals the coin. Two states
  only: **whole** (open, standing, kept, or not-your-fault deaths) and **scored /
  wounded** (only for promises you personally failed — abandoned).

### Metal = weight tier
| Tier | Metal | Meaning |
|------|-------|---------|
| Ordinary | Aluminum (lightest) | everyday commitments |
| Serious | Brass | the ones that cost you something |
| Standing | Copper (heaviest) | forever promises |

### Aging as a record of time (design principle)
The **aluminum (ordinary) discs are deliberately left bare** — never waxed or
sealed — so they dull and age over the years. The loss of shine becomes a visible
record of **how long the weight has been carried**: a freshly-bound ordinary
promise is bright; an old one is dull and handled. Time made visible on the metal.

**Brass (serious) and copper (standing) are preserved** with Renaissance Wax — so
the heavier the promise, the more it's protected against decay. The gradient of
weight becomes a gradient of permanence: ordinary promises weather freely, serious
ones are kept, and the forever-promises are protected most. They still warm and
deepen slowly in tone under the wax; the wax only blocks the ugly failure modes
(green copper corrosion, blotchy brass tarnish).

**Waxing method (brass + copper only):**
1. Clean and **fully dry** the disc — any moisture or fingerprint oil sealed under
   the wax becomes permanent.
2. Pick up a fingertip's worth of wax (it liquefies at skin temperature) and spread
   a **very thin** coat over the whole disc, including into the engraved grooves
   where corrosion otherwise collects. Thin is the whole point — thick coats look
   cloudy.
3. Let it haze for a minute or two, then **buff to a soft sheen** with a clean cloth.
4. **Reapply** every several months to once a year (less for discs resting in a jar)
   — the wax is a sacrificial layer that carries soiling away when re-buffed.

Do this once when the disc is first made and stamped, then on the periodic-review
cadence. Never wax the aluminum.

> Honest note on the metal: aluminum forms a clear oxide and tends to *dull* rather
> than darken dramatically, so the aging is subtle (greyer cast, fine scratches,
> lost shine) rather than a strong patina. Handling the older discs — skin oils —
> speeds the dulling, so the promises you return to most will show their age most.

### The four jars + the two separate vessels
- **Open · Standing · Kept · Dead (Broken)** — the four jars.
- **Dead-promises jar** (the UV-black one) holds failed promises — *one-way*, never
  resurrected.
- **Memorial vessel** (separate, to be chosen) holds memorial discs for people who
  have died — kept apart from the dead-promises jar, because the dead are not
  failures.

### How a disc is made (workflow)
1. Make the promise in the app → it assigns the label, metal, and (when you set
   them) the keyword and fate-glyph.
2. Grab a blank in that metal. **Stamp the label** on the front (`M-047`).
3. **Hand-engrave the keyword** above the number, and the **fate-glyph** around the
   label (the app's disc-maker tells you which glyph).
4. On a favor: also hand-engrave the **verification number** on the front.
5. **Hand-engrave the maker's mark** on the back (always engraved, never stamped).
   If the promise died by your own hand, **score the mark.**
6. Ink the stamped grooves, wipe the face.
7. Hit **Mark stamped** in the app, and drop the disc in its jar.
8. **Promotion:** if an Open promise becomes Standing, its disc may be in a lighter
   metal — the app flags it "re-forge in copper." Re-stamp a copper disc and retire
   the old one, or keep the original as a record of where it began.

> Practice on aluminum before real brass/copper — first strikes are always uneven.
> One firm strike per character, not several taps.

## 2. Upgrades (as the practice deepens)

No rush — live with the starter kit first; you'll learn what actually annoys you.
Costs are rough and general.

### Durability & finishing
- **Protective wax for copper/brass** (microcrystalline / Renaissance-type wax) —
  *optional.* Stabilizes the serious and standing tiers if you want them to weather
  slowly and stay legible. **Do not wax the aluminum** — it's meant to age. ~$15–25,
  lasts years. (Or skip entirely and let every metal weather, if you want the whole
  set to record time.)
- **Deburring/finishing** — a small file or deburring tool to clean blank edges
  before stamping. ~$10–20.

### Stamping quality & consistency
- **A stamping jig / alignment guide** — holds the disc and aligns punches so
  `M-001` comes out straight and evenly spaced. The single biggest quality upgrade;
  hand-aligning is the thing that frustrates people first. ~$15–40.
- **Arbor press (small)** — replaces the hammer for *identical* strike depth every
  time. Matters for the engraving-vs-stamping forgery logic on favors, and for a
  uniform look across decades. ~$40–80.
- **Refined brass hammer** (replaceable-face, ergonomic) for cleaner, squarer
  strikes if you stay with hand-stamping. ~$25–45.
- **Larger / deeper letter set, or a second size** if you want hierarchy (e.g. a
  bigger size for the keystone). ~$15–30.

### Engraving (keyword, glyph, verification, and the seal)
- **Micro-motor / rotary engraver** — more control than a diamond scribe for the
  fate-glyphs and the maker's-mark seal (all of which you hand-engrave). ~$20–60
  cordless; more for a bench micro-motor.
- **Engraving practice plates** (cheap brass/aluminum offcuts) — get the seal and
  glyphs fluent before committing them to real discs. ~$5–15.

> The maker's mark is **always hand-engraved**, never a bought punch — so there's no
> custom-stamp purchase in this list. Your hand is the un-buyable layer.

### The vessels
- **A matched set** if you want the living three (and/or all four) to read as a
  family — heavier ground-glass-stopper apothecary jars, or a full Infinity Jars
  set. The current mason + single UV-black mix works and is arguably more meaningful
  (the dead jar apart), so this is aesthetic, not necessary. ~$30–60 for a decent
  set; a premium all-glass apothecary set runs into the hundreds.
- **A dedicated memorial vessel** — currently unbought. Something distinct from both
  the dead jar and the living jars: a small lidded box, a lantern, an urn. ~$15–40.
- **A wax seal or harder-to-open lid for the dead jar** — friction that makes
  resurrection feel as wrong physically as it is in the system. ~$10–20.

### Carrying & ritual frame
- **A single dedicated box / case** that holds punches, hammer, blanks, engraver,
  wax, and a strike pad — so *opening the box is the threshold* of the ritual,
  giving the practice a physical frame. The upgrade I'd value most after durability.
  ~$25–60.
- **Tool roll** for the punches (protects the faces, suits the ritual feel). ~$15–25.
- **Leather strike pad** — quiets the strike and protects surfaces away from home.
  ~$10–15.
- **Travel-light variant** — a smaller 2–3" block + lighter hammer for binding
  promises away from home, so the practice never breaks when you travel. ~$20–35.

### Extra heft (optional)
- **Steel washers** dropped into a jar if you want more physical weight beyond what
  the metals give. ~$3–5.

## 3. Buy-before-you-stamp reminders
- **Practice on aluminum** before real brass/copper — first strikes are uneven.
- One **firm strike** per character, not several taps.
- Confirm any new punch/stamp is **rated for brass and copper**, not just aluminum.
- **Standardize disc size** (1") if you want a uniform set.
- Glass is glass — wrap jars when traveling.
