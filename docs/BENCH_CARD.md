# The Weight — Bench Reference Card

A concise at-the-bench checklist for making discs. Keep it with the kit.

> First dozen discs will be uneven — that's normal, not failure. Practice on
> aluminum first; save the copper keystone for after your strikes are clean.

---

## Setup (before any strike)

- **Hard, heavy backing.** Steel bench block on a solid table, ideally over a leg —
  not a flexing surface. A leather pad/sandbag *under* the block deadens ring and
  protects the table, but the block needs hard backing beneath it.
- **Secure the blank.** It must not move. Painter's tape over the blank (stamp
  through it — also helps spacing) or a jig. Movement = the #1 cause of double-strikes.
- **Punch vertical.** Hold it 90° to the disc, no tilt. Tilt = half the letter deep,
  half faint.

## The strike (the whole game)

- **ONE firm strike per character. Never several taps.** Tapping shifts the punch and
  ghosts the letter. One confident blow seats the whole character.
- **No bounce.** Grip firmly so the punch can't rebound and re-hit.
- **Let the hammer's weight work.** Relaxed, heavier swing beats a tense hard one.
- **Harder metals need more force.** Aluminum is easy; brass and copper need a firmer
  strike for the same depth.
- **Brass hammer note:** a brass face disperses the blow evenly — cleaner single
  strikes, far less rebound than steel, and it protects your punches. The single-
  firm-strike rule still applies, but the tool is working with you here.

## Layout & spacing

- Use the bench block's ruler lines (or a tape strip) as a level baseline.
- **Center-out** for centered text: middle character first, then alternate sides.
- **Dry-run the layout** — lay the punches on the blank without striking to check it
  fits before committing.
- Front format: `M-001` (or O-/F-) on top, **keyword beneath**.

## Finishing

- Rub enamel marker/ink into the grooves, let set, wipe the flat surface clean. Ink
  stays in the recesses; the label reads dark.

---

## Practice plan before the keystone

1. Aluminum: stamp single letters, then `M-001`, then the keyword, until strikes are
   even and single-impression.
2. One or two practice strikes on a **brass** blank to feel the extra force.
3. Then stamp the real **copper keystone**, deliberately, warmed up.

---

## Favor tokens — extra steps

Favor discs (front label `F-###`) carry two things normal discs don't:

**1. Maker's mark (front).**
- Stamp/press your hand-made maker's mark beneath the `F-###` label.
- It's the un-buyable layer — the mark only you have. Keep its design private.

**2. Verification number (back) — HAND-ENGRAVE, don't stamp.**
- The app generates a unique **10-digit number** per favor; it shows on the entry and
  the stamping worklist ("back: 4829153607").
- Engrave it on the **back** with the engraving pen — hand engraving (vs. stamping)
  is part of what makes it hard to forge.
- This number is checked when the favor is redeemed; a wrong number flags a forgery.

**Favor disc summary:**
- Front: `F-###` + keyword (optional) + your maker's mark.
- Back: the 10-digit verification number, hand-engraved.
- Metal: Aluminum (ordinary) or Brass (serious) — favors are never copper/standing.

## Quick metal → tier reminder

| Tier | Metal | For |
|------|-------|-----|
| Ordinary | Aluminum | everyday promises |
| Serious | Brass | promises that cost something |
| Standing | Copper | forever promises (heaviest) |

Favors use Aluminum or Brass only.
