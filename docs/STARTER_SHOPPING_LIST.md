# The Weight — Starter Shopping List (~$100–150)

A check-off list to get the physical system running, with real products and rough
prices. This is the budget starter; upgrade the jars and tools later if the
practice sticks.

> I can't see live prices or stock — **verify every listing before buying.** Kit
> contents and pack counts vary between sellers even when photos look identical.
> Confirm any stamping tool is rated for **brass and copper** (your harder metals).

---

## ☐ 1. Stamping kit — stamp + block (the base) — ~$30

A complete kit so you get number/letter punches and a steel bench block in one box.
- **74-piece Metal Stamping Kit (DkOvn / generic)** — 3mm punches incl. A-Z, a-z,
  0-9, &, symbols, plus a steel bench block (and a hammer you'll replace — see #2).
  Gives you the **M, O, 0-9** you need.
  - amazon.com/dp/B0D4TKX14K  (~$30)
  - Alt: "74pcs Metal Stamping Kit, DkOvn" (Amazon Best Sellers) — includes a
    stamping jig that helps alignment.
- Confirm it lists brass/copper among supported metals (the 74pc kits do).

> You're buying this mainly for the **punches + bench block**. The kit's steel
> hammer works, but you're replacing it with something better and more fitting ↓

---

## ☐ 2. The hammer — dark & symbolic — ~$25–45

Skip the plastic kit hammer. A **hand-forged, black ball-peen blacksmith hammer
with a wood handle** suits the ritual far better — dark head, real heft, the tool
of someone who makes permanent marks. The ball (domed) end is also genuinely
useful; the flat face does your stamping.

- **Picard English-pattern ball-peen, black powder-coated head, ash handle** —
  c45 alloy steel, hardened & tempered, chamfered face.
  - blacksmithsource.com (Picard engineer/blacksmith ball-peen) — ~$30–45
- **Forged ball-peen, blackened head, oiled ash handle** (compact ~2 lb) — can be
  hand-engraved on the handle if you want to make it truly yours.
  - ancientsmithy.com/products/ball-peen-hammer-for-blacksmithing-ykhamm-30
- **Budget/vintage:** a used black ball-peen with a wood handle on eBay/Etsy
  ("hand forged ball peen hammer wood handle") — ~$20–35, often with patina.

> Weight note: for stamping small discs you want roughly **1–1.5 lb** for control.
> The 2-3 lb blacksmith hammers are heavier than needed but still work with a
> lighter tap; if you want the easiest stamping, look for the lighter end. Darker
> look vs. ideal weight is the one tradeoff here — a blackened ~1 lb ball-peen is
> the sweet spot if you can find it.

## ☐ 3. The three metals (discs) — ~$30–35 total

Round coin blanks, ~1", ~14 gauge, one metal per weight tier. ABBECIAO sells all
three in matching coin form (abbeciao.com):
- **Aluminum** (ordinary) — "1"× 1/8" Heavy Gauge Aluminum Coin Blanks, deburred"
  — from ~$11.99. Your most-used + practice metal.
  - Cheaper bulk alt: **RMP 1" round aluminum, 14 ga., 50-pack** — amazon.com/dp/B00FY3ZYT0
- **Brass** (serious) — "25mm 16ga Round Brass Tags," packs of 5/21/100 — ~$8–13.
- **Copper** (standing) — "1" Round Copper Stamping Blanks, 14ga, pack of 20" — ~$10–13.

> Buy a few **extra aluminum** to practice on before your first real brass/copper.

## ☐ 4. Custom maker's mark (for favor tokens) — ~$30–40

A single custom steel punch cut with your own unique glyph/monogram — the
un-buyable mark that makes favor discs hard to forge. You upload a 1-color design;
they cut a hardened steel stamp.
- **SozDat custom steel logo stamp** — upload B&W/vector art; hardened 54-58 HRC;
  pick rod size (a ~6-10mm face suits a disc). ~$36.
  - sozdat.com/products/custom-metal-stamp  (also on eBay, item 304616908525)
- **Custom Logo Metal Stamp (Amazon)** — carbon steel U8A, made to your logo.
  - amazon.com/dp/B0CNQC9Q1D
- **Premium / US-made:** Buckeye Engraving (buckeyeengraving.com) — higher quality,
  higher price; a later upgrade if you want it.

> Only needed if you're doing favor tokens. Stamp it on the front of each favor
> disc, beneath the F-### label (the app shows where it goes).

---

## ☐ 5. Engraving pen (for favor verification numbers) — ~$10–20

A diamond-tip or rotary engraving pen to **hand-engrave** the app-generated
10-digit verification number on the **back** of each favor disc. Hand engraving
(vs. stamping) is part of what makes a forgery hard.
- Search Amazon: **"diamond tip engraving pen"** (~$10) or **"rotary engraver
  cordless"** (~$15-20). Either works on aluminum/brass/copper.

> Only needed for favor tokens. For non-favor discs you never need this.

---

## ☐ 6. The four jars — ~$25–35

Three matched **clear cork-stopper** jars (Open / Standing / Kept) + one **amber
cork** jar (Broken). Wide-mouth, same size for the three clear ones.
- Search: "glass jars with cork lids" (eBay / Walmart / Target), 4-pack or singles
  to match; one amber bottle/jar with cork for Broken.

---

## ☐ 7. Ink for the stamped grooves — $0–8

Rub into stamped letters, wipe the face, label stays dark.
- If your #1 kit includes **enamel markers**, this is covered ($0).
- Else: an enamel marker (~$8) or a Sharpie as a free stopgap.

### Trimming to stay at/under ~$130
If you want to start cheaper and add favor tokens later:
- **Defer #4 (maker's mark, ~$35) and #5 (engraving pen, ~$15)** until you actually
  start handing out favors. That drops you to **~$100–120** for the core promise
  system, and you add the favor-token gear when you're ready.
- Or pick the **vintage ~$20 hammer** instead of a new forged one to save ~$15–25.

> The maker's mark + engraving pen are *only* needed for favor tokens. If favors
> aren't day-one, skip #4 and #5 and you're solidly in your $100–150 window with
> room to spare.

## Don't-forget reminders
- Confirm the stamping kit handles **brass and copper**, not just aluminum.
- Match the **three clear jars by size**; make the fourth **amber** for Broken.
- Favor a **wide-mouth** jar — you'll add/remove discs constantly.
- **Practice on aluminum** before real brass/copper — first strikes are uneven.
- A ~**1–1.5 lb** hammer stamps small discs more easily than a heavy 2-3 lb one.
