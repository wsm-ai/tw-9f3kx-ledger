# The Weight — The Rituals

The ceremonies that turn a tracked promise into a bound one. Ritual creates a
clear before/after threshold, engages the hands so the commitment is encoded
deeply, and gives the act weight. One ritual, performed the same way every time —
so the practice itself becomes a path walked over and over.

> Principle: the same ritual serves the heaviest copper keystone and the lightest
> aluminum promise. The weight scales through the **metal and the words**, never by
> adding steps. Keep it short enough to repeat willingly; let gravity come from
> content, not length.

---

## The Binding — for every promise

One ritual, every promise, ~under a minute. Seven beats in a single arc:
**gather → frame → vow → seal → vow → frame → rest.**

1. **The pause.** Hold the blank disc. Be still for a breath or two. This is the
   threshold — nothing is committed yet; it's your last honest chance not to bind.

2. **The opening line** (fixed, said every time):
   > *"What I speak, I will carry."*

3. **Speak the vow** — the full sentence aloud (not just the keyword).
   e.g. the keystone: *"I will do what I believe is right, and bear the cost of it."*

4. **The strike.** Stamp the disc. This is the binding — the irreversible mark. The
   sound of the strike is the seal beginning.

5. **Speak the vow again** — over the finished disc. *This is what I have bound.*

6. **The closing line** (fixed, said every time):
   > *"I will bear it."*

7. **Place.** Set the disc in its jar. Bound and at rest.

**The frame's meaning:** the opening names the burden *before* it's taken; the
closing accepts the weight *after* it's bound. Both are weight-language — fitting for
*The Weight*. The vow spoken on both sides of the strike means you declare your intent,
seal it, then affirm what you've done.

## Notes

- **Keystone first.** When materials arrive, the keystone (M-001 · CODE) is bound
  alone, as the founding act — the first walk of the path. Take the pause longer than
  usual; everything after answers to this one.
- **Favors:** the Binding still applies when you make a favor token. The maker's mark
  and the hand-engraved verification number are part of the strike step (step 4).
- **Other moments** (keeping, breaking, promoting, redeeming, death, review) may earn
  their own rituals later — designed one at a time, the way the glyph language grows.
  For now, only the Binding is fixed.
