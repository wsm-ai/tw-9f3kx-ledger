# The Weight — Symbolism

The canonical record of what every element of the project *means*. Where
`FATE_LANGUAGE.md` holds the script, `RITUALS.md` the ceremonies, and
`PHYSICAL_BUILD.md` the build, this holds the **why** — the symbolic logic that ties
all the layers into one system.

> Living document. Update this whenever a meaning-bearing decision is made, so the
> symbolism never drifts out of sync with the build, the app, or the language.

---

## The core metaphor: weight

Every promise is a weight you choose to take onto yourself. The system makes that
weight **physical** — a metal disc you stamp, hold, and store — and **visible** — out
on a shelf, the heaviest jar holding the heaviest promises. The name is literal: the
app shows the grams you carry right now. To promise is to pick something up; to keep
or break it is to finally set it down.

---

## The two halves: making vs. keeping

The system splits cleanly into two acts:

- **The tools** — in a carrying box that is itself a ritual threshold — are for
  **making**: the active act of binding a promise. Opening the box means *I am about
  to bind something.* The box holds only what the ritual needs (punches, hammer,
  block, engraver, ink, wax, blanks) — never the jars.
- **The jars** — out on a shelf, visible — are for **keeping**: the standing record.

One is a verb, one a noun. They are never stored together, because making and keeping
are different acts.

---

## The metals — weight as permanence

Three tiers, and the metal itself carries the meaning:

| Tier | Metal | Meaning | Aging |
|------|-------|---------|-------|
| Ordinary | **Aluminum** (lightest) | everyday commitments | left **bare** — dulls and ages |
| Serious | **Brass** | the ones that cost you something | **waxed**, preserved |
| Standing | **Copper** (heaviest) | forever promises, no end | **waxed**, made to endure |

The gradient isn't only weight — it's **permanence**. Aluminum is deliberately left
unsealed so it dulls over the years: an old ordinary promise visibly shows how long
the weight has been carried. Brass and copper are sealed with Renaissance Wax to age
slowly and gracefully without corroding. So ordinary promises wear their age openly,
while the serious and standing ones are protected to last.

**Material becomes meaning: what ages, and what is kept whole.**

## The two faces of a disc

- **Front — the fate.** The identity label (`M-###` to self, `O-###` to other,
  `F-###` favor), the **keyword** hand-engraved above the number, and the
  **fate-glyph** engraved around it. The public face: who the promise is to, and what
  became of it.
- **Back — the maker's mark.** Your sigil, **always hand-engraved, never stamped** —
  your hand is the un-buyable thing. It seals the coin and (on favors) makes forgery
  impossible. Two states only:
  - **Whole** — open, standing, kept, or a death that was not your fault.
  - **Scored / wounded** — only for promises you personally failed (abandoned).

---

## The vessels — each a character

Transparent vessels for the living; opaque for the dead. They are intentionally
*not* a matched set — each carries its own character, the way the app's jars do.

- **Open** — an unsealed working **bowl** (ceramic). Promises in motion, handled
  constantly, the least precious vessel. Nothing here is finished. When a promise is
  kept, its disc *graduates* from this humble bowl into the bright glass of Kept.
- **Standing** — a heavy **pillar**. Immovable, enshrined, the forever promises; the
  most substantial vessel on the shelf.
- **Kept** — a raised **chalice**, lifted and bright. Your proof you follow through;
  the one vessel allowed to be beautiful.
- **Dead** — a sealed, **opaque** jar, set apart, hard to open. Dead is one-way; the
  vessel makes resurrection feel as wrong as it is in the system.
- **Memorial** — a **lantern**, not a jar. For people who have died — the dead kept
  in *light*, never mistakable for the dead-*promises* jar, because the dead are not
  failures. Different in form entirely; that separation is doctrinally essential.

## The fate-language — borrowed meanings, one hand

A personal mythic script for recording what became of a promise. The founding
principle: **borrow meanings, not symbols** — draw from Norse, Greek, Egyptian, and
alchemical sources, but render everything in one consistent hand, so the system is
*yours*, not a scrapbook.

**The grammar:**
- **Base stroke = the fate** — kept is an enclosing **ring** (came full circle);
  broken is a severing **slash** (cut, let go).
- **Stroke weight = severity.**
- **The glyph = the circumstance** (the part that grows).
- **Straight strokes = the fixed/binding; curved strokes = the living/cyclical.**

**The maker's mark — "The Eternal Path":** an S-lemniscate threading two ouroboros
turnings inside a wholeness circle. It sets the visual hand for the whole language
and means the practice itself: bind, keep or break, mark, repeat — for life.

---

## The fates a promise can meet

- **Kept** — came full circle (the ring). Refined by *how*: at great sacrifice,
  easily / naturally, long overdue, or honored though they are gone.
- **Dead** — and *why* matters morally:
  - **Abandoned** — you let it fall. Your fault (the wounded sigil).
  - **Taken** — ended by something outside you. Not your fault (the whole sigil).
  Agency is the distinction the language insists on.
- **Death** — a promise touched by the death of the person it was made to: Charon's
  obol meets the setting sun, a coin paid as passage. Never filed as a failure.

## The keystone

**M-001**, the founding promise, bound first and alone as the founding act:

> *"I will do what I believe is right, and bear the cost of it."*

It judges all the others: when you break any promise, the keystone's vow surfaces as
the standard you are measured against. Integrity as wholeness — and the wholeness
circle that frames the entire language echoes it.

---

## Favors — debts you choose to owe

Bearer tokens you hand out: an IOU on a disc, meaning "the holder is owed one favor."
Transferable, so they **outlive** whoever you gave them to — a favor survives its
origin, and so has no death-fate. Protected by your hand-engraved maker's mark plus a
10-digit verification number that must match your ledger: only you can confirm a
token is genuine and unspent.

## The rituals

One ceremony turns a tracked promise into a bound one. The weight scales through the
**metal and the words**, never by adding steps.

**The Binding** — every promise, ~one minute:
pause (the last honest chance not to bind) → *"What I speak, I will carry"* → speak
the vow → **the strike** (the irreversible seal) → speak the vow again → *"I will bear
it"* → place it in the jar. The opening names the burden *before* it's taken; the
closing accepts the weight *after*.

**The Keeping** — an exhale to the Binding's inhale; past-tense, the promise come
full circle.

**The Breaking** — branched between a chosen break (you own it) and a taken break
(mourning), closing: *"The thread is cut. My hands are empty — and where it was cut, I
do not heal."*

---

## Time, made visible

The aluminum dulls; the copper may patina. The app's **Ledger** charts what you've
taken on, kept, and let die over the years, and a **reckoning** surfaces promises gone
quiet for 90+ days. The whole system is built to be **read backward someday** — a life
recorded in metal, each disc carrying its story on its two faces.

---

## The throughline

**Meaning made physical and permanent** — weight you can hold, fate you can read, time
you can see, and a record that will outlast the remembering.
